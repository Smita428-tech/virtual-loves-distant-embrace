A Poem for Missing Loved Ones

In days of old, when distance stretched its hand,

And loved ones dwelled in distant, far-off lands,

The ache of separation gripped the heart,

With no swift means to bridge the miles apart.

Oh, how they yearned, those souls of yore,

To touch a cheek, to hold one close once more,

No screens to flicker faces, bright and clear,

No virtual embrace to quell their tears.

They wrote their words upon parchment dear,

In ink-stained letters, whispers of love sincere,

Each stroke of pen a bridge, though fragile, true,

A lifeline woven through the ocean's blue.

Their hearts, resilient, forged in iron and fire,

Withstanding time, distance, and its cruel attire,

They longed for a touch, a presence near,

While destiny played games, their souls held dear.

Yet let us cherish this digital age we live,

Where love can reach across the globe, forgive

The limitations that once bound our fate,

Now memories and moments need not wait.

For in this era, blessed with wondrous charms,

We hold the power to connect, in each other's arms,

Through screens and pixels, love's song still sings,

Though miles may part, our hearts take flight on wings.

So, as you miss your friend, so far away,

Remember, you're blessed with means to convey

Your love and longing through the virtual sphere,

And cherish the gift of connection, my dear.

In every touch of key, every virtual embrace,

Let gratitude bloom, filling every space,

For those who came before, their hearts held fast,

And paved the way for this love to last.

In this vast expanse of digital domain,

May love's sweet presence forever remain,

And in your heart, hold dear the joys anew,

For bridging distances, connecting me to you.
